Intuition

A common agent shape that splits one hard job into three roles: a planner decomposes the goal into steps, an executor carries out each step against the environment, and a verifier checks whether the result is actually correct before moving on. Separating “decide / do / check” lets each part be optimized (or swapped) independently and makes failures legible.

Details

The verifier is the load-bearing piece: a cheap, reliable check (tests, a reward model, a rule) that gates progress and enables search (retry/branch on failure). In SWE agents the verifier is often the repo’s own test suite (execution-based eval); in web/GUI agents it may be a learned outcome judge. Without a trustworthy verifier, inference-time search and self-correction degrade into guesswork.

Q&A

Why is the verifier the key part? Because it converts an open-ended generation problem into a checkable one — you can afford a weak planner/executor if you can reliably tell good from bad and retry.
