<TLDR>AGUVIS is the first fully autonomous, pure-vision GUI agent: a single Qwen2-VL-based model that operates directly on screenshots with a unified `pyautogui` action space and explicit inner-monologue reasoning, reaching state-of-the-art on offline and online benchmarks without any closed-source model in the loop.</TLDR>

## Motivation
Autonomous GUI automation has been stuck against four coupled limitations. <Problem>Most agents consume textual interface representations (HTML or accessibility trees), which run past 4K tokens and grow with interface complexity</Problem>, raising cost and hurting generalization. <Problem>Heterogeneous, platform-specific action spaces fragment training data across web/mobile/desktop and block cross-environment learning</Problem>. <Problem>Methods either lack reliable visual grounding or outsource reasoning to closed-source LLMs</Problem>, creating a bottleneck that cannot be improved by training. And <Problem>agents are typically trained to emit reactive low-level actions directly, failing to exploit the planning capacity of VLMs</Problem>.

## Contribution (what's new)
The genuine delta is a single open model that is both grounder and planner on vision alone. Three design choices make it work: <Novelty>a unified `pyautogui`-based action space with a plugin layer for platform-specific gestures, so one model's "thought-to-action" mapping transfers across web, mobile, and desktop</Novelty>; <Novelty>explicit two-component inner monologue (a free-form thought plus a low-level natural-language instruction) baked into training data, turning reactive action prediction into deliberate planning</Novelty>; and <Novelty>a two-stage pipeline that first trains grounding on single screenshots, then trains planning/reasoning on inner-monologue trajectories</Novelty>. The vision-only observation is what permits the unified action space: pixel coordinates are a common substrate across platforms in a way HTML/accessibility trees are not.

## Research questions
Can an open-weights, pure-vision model perform both grounding and high-level planning end-to-end across platforms, matching or beating pipelines that depend on closed-source planners and textual inputs — and does explicit inner-monologue reasoning, plus cross-platform unification, measurably help?

## Methodology
AGUVIS finetunes Qwen2-VL (a NaViT-style dynamic-resolution encoder with 2D-RoPE, chosen for native variable-resolution screenshot handling) on a purpose-built dataset, then evaluates as either a standalone agent or a grounder paired with a GPT-4o planner.

The inner monologue at inference has two parts per step: an explicit thought `h_t` conditioned on the goal `G` and previous thought `h_{t-1}`, then a low-level natural-language action instruction that is translated into a concrete `pyautogui` command. The framework's model-agnostic claim is checked by re-implementing on LLaVA-OneVision (§4.1).
</Pass>

## Key findings
- <Finding src="Tab.2">On Multimodal-Mind2Web, AGUVIS lifts Step Success Rate by +51.9% on average over prior methods</Finding>: the largest relative gains are in planning-heavy step success, not just element accuracy, indicating the reasoning stage, not only grounding, drives the win.
- <Finding src="Tab.8">Despite being trained only on web and mobile data, AGUVIS generalizes to desktop OSWorld — 17.04% (GPT-4o planner + AGUVIS-7B) vs 4.59% for SoM and 14.9% for Claude Computer-Use; standalone AGUVIS-72B reaches 10.26%</Finding>: evidence it captures transferable GUI principles rather than overfitting environments.
- <Finding src="Tab.6">Both stages matter: removing Stage 2 (planning) drops ScreenSpot 84.4→81.8 and MM-Mind2Web Cross-Task 60.4→50.9; removing Stage 1 (grounding) drops ScreenSpot 84.4→77.4; removing both collapses ScreenSpot to 55.3</Finding>.
- <Finding src="Tab.6">Inner monologue helps low-level execution, not just planning: removing it drops ScreenSpot 84.4→79.3 and AndroidControl low-level 80.5→69.1</Finding> — a counterintuitive result, since one might expect reasoning to aid only high-level steps.
- <Finding src="Tab.7">Unified cross-platform training beats platform-specific training: web+mobile data (35K traj.) scores 58.5 / 55.4 / 54.8 on MM-Mind2Web Task/Website/Domain vs 53.1 / 50.3 / 52.2 for web-only (6K)</Finding>, supporting the shared-representation hypothesis behind the unified action space.
- <Finding src="§4.4">Pure vision cuts cost sharply: a constant 1,196 input tokens/step for 720p images vs ~4K for HTML, giving 93% cost reduction and 70% fewer input tokens/step vs GPT-4o on Mind2Web-Live</Finding>; cost-per-successful-step falls to $0.012 for AGUVIS-72B vs $0.142 for GPT-4o (Tab.4).
- <Finding src="Tab.15">The pipeline is largely model-agnostic: on a weaker LLaVA backbone, ScreenSpot rises from 3.8% to 81.2% after full AGUVIS training</Finding>, attributing gains to the method rather than to Qwen2-VL alone.

## Implications
This reframes the computer-use-agent stack around an open, vision-only model that is simultaneously grounder and planner, undercutting the dominant <Related>GPT-4o-planner + separate-grounder pipelines such as UGround, SeeClick, and OmniParser</Related> on both accuracy and cost. It extends the lineage of <Related>vision-grounding work (SeeClick, UGround) and reasoning-augmented agents (inner monologue, Huang et al. 2022)</Related> into a single trainable artifact, and the open-sourced datasets/recipes lower the barrier for the field. Threads worth chasing: <FollowUp>whether the inner-monologue augmentation can be regenerated with open VLMs instead of GPT-4o, removing the last closed-source dependency in the data pipeline (authors flag this as future work)</FollowUp>; <FollowUp>adding refusal/uncertainty behavior so agents can decline ambiguous or unsafe actions</FollowUp>; and <FollowUp>a dynamic "when to plan" threshold to balance reasoning overhead against accuracy</FollowUp>.

## Limitations
- <Caveat>The planning/reasoning training data depends on GPT-4o to synthesize inner monologues, so the "no closed-source model" claim holds at inference but not for data construction</Caveat>.
- <Caveat>The agent cannot express uncertainty or refuse ambiguous instructions; in a 50-sample ScreenSpot error analysis, 40% of failures stem from ambiguous instructions and 60% from grounding errors (§4.5, Fig.4)</Caveat>.
- <Caveat>Standalone AGUVIS-72B underperforms its own GPT-4o-paired 7B configuration on some online tasks (e.g. AndroidWorld 26.1 vs 37.1, Tab.5), so the unified model is not yet uniformly the best deployment</Caveat>.
- <Caveat>Online success rates are low in absolute terms (Mind2Web-Live 27.1%, OSWorld 17.04%), so real-world reliability remains far from usable</Caveat>.
- <Caveat>Desktop generalization is shown only via OSWorld with no desktop training data; broad desktop competence is inferred, not directly trained or measured at scale</Caveat>.

## Critiques and Questions
1. The +51.9% Step-SR headline (Tab.2) is a relative average over heterogeneous baselines; the absolute Cross-Task gap is 64.0 vs 40.2, which is strong but less dramatic than the framing suggests — worth reading per-cell rather than as one number.
2. Numbers appear to be single runs with no variance/error bars reported; given small online benchmarks (e.g. Mind2Web-Live, OSWorld's 369 tasks), the ~3-point online margins over baselines may be within noise.
3. Self-plan vs original-instruction ScreenSpot results are mixed in the table; it is worth confirming which setting each cited number comes from, since the unified action space's advantage could partly reflect evaluation-protocol choices.

## Glossary
- <Term def="A purely vision-based GUI agent: it reads the screen as pixels and outputs interface actions, with no HTML or accessibility-tree input.">pure-vision agent</Term>
- <Term def="Two-part per-step reasoning trace — a free-form thought plus a low-level natural-language action instruction — generated before the concrete action, to make planning explicit.">inner monologue</Term>
- <Term def="Mapping a natural-language instruction to the correct on-screen element/coordinate to act on.">grounding</Term>
- <Term def="A Python automation library whose commands (click, write, press, dragTo) AGUVIS adopts as its universal, cross-platform action vocabulary.">pyautogui</Term>
- <Term def="Cross-platform GUI grounding benchmark spanning mobile, desktop, and web; here the headline 89.2% average accuracy.">ScreenSpot</Term>
- <Term def="Benchmark of 369 real-world computer tasks across web apps, desktop software, and OS operations; used here to test desktop generalization.">OSWorld</Term>
- <Term def="Set-of-Marks — overlaying numbered marks on candidate UI elements so a model can refer to them by index; a grounding alternative AGUVIS outperforms.">SoM</Term>
- <Term def="Training trick that packs multiple instruction-action pairs from one screenshot into a single example (single-image-multiple-turn) to reduce redundant grounding data.">grounding packing</Term>
