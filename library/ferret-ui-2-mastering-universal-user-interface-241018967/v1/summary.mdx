<TLDR>Ferret-UI 2 is a single multimodal LLM for fine-grained UI referring, grounding, and user-centered interaction across five platforms (iPhone, Android, iPad, Webpage, AppleTV), built from <Novelty>adaptive grid-based high-resolution encoding plus GPT-4o set-of-mark visual prompting for training-data generation</Novelty>.</TLDR>

<WhyItMatters>A foundational computer-use-agent perception model: it shows how to make one MLLM understand UIs at native resolution across heterogeneous device types, and that better visually-grounded training data — not just architecture — drives the gains.</WhyItMatters>

## Motivation
Prior UI MLLMs are <Problem>locked to one or two platform types</Problem> — the original Ferret-UI handles only mobile (iPhone/Android). They also use a <Problem>fixed grounding resolution (336×672 / 672×336)</Problem>, which distorts the diverse native resolutions and aspect ratios of iPad, Web, and (landscape) AppleTV screens. Finally, training data was generated with <Problem>text-only GPT-4 prompting where bounding boxes are pure text</Problem>, stripping out the visual and spatial relationships between widgets and capping the quality of the resulting supervision.

## Contribution (what's new)
Three coupled deltas. (1) <Novelty>Adaptive N-gridding</Novelty>: instead of unbounded any-resolution tiling, search for the grid `(Nw, Nh)` that minimizes aspect-ratio distortion times relative pixel change under a cost cap `Nw + Nh ≤ N` — preserving native resolution within a fixed inference budget. (2) <Novelty>GPT-4o with set-of-mark (SoM) visual prompting</Novelty> to author advanced-task data: each widget is drawn with a corner-style, color-coded numbered box on the actual screenshot, so the generator reasons over real spatial layout rather than text coordinates. This is needed because text-only prompting cannot perceive widget appearance or nesting, and plain GPT-4o has poor grounding. (3) A <Novelty>uniform 13-class label space and multi-platform dataset</Novelty> built largely from human/HTML annotations rather than model-detected boxes.

## Research questions
Can one MLLM deliver fine-grained referring, grounding, and user-centered single-step interaction across structurally different platforms; how much of the gain comes from architecture vs. data quality; and does the model transfer zero-shot to unseen platforms and benchmarks?

## Methodology
Build on Ferret-UI's CLIP ViT-L/14 + Visual-Sampler + LLM architecture (tried Gemma-2B, Llama3-8B, Vicuna-13B). Collect screenshots per platform (human boxes for Apple devices, parsed HTML hierarchy for Web/WebUI, RICO for Android; OCR fills missing text). Construct elementary tasks (3 referring + 3 grounding) directly from annotations, and 3 advanced task types via GPT-4o+SoM, then train one joint model.

## Main result
On the authors' constructed benchmarks (45 = 9 subtasks × 5 platforms, averaged), Ferret-UI 2 with Llama3-8B reaches an <Finding src="Tab.2">advanced-task GPT-4o score of 89.73, beating Ferret-UI's 45.81 by 43.92 points and GPT-4o's 77.73 by 12.0 points</Finding>, plus the best <Finding src="Tab.2">GUIDE next-action IoU of 55.78 vs 26.91 for Ferret-UI</Finding>. GPT-4o is strong only when given SoM prompting on referring (87.91) but collapses on grounding (12.14 elementary, 7.06 advanced Multi-IoU).

## Key findings
- <Finding src="Tab.5">Data quality matters more than architecture</Finding>: on the harder iPhone-v2 test set, the new dataset lifts the GPT-4o score from 85.97 to 89.7 (+3.73), whereas Ferret-UI 2's architecture adds only +1.9 on the same data; on the easy iPhone-v1 set both contribute marginally — the improved visually-grounded data is the dominant lever.
- <Finding src="Tab.3">Strong zero-shot generalization</Finding>: on GUI-World, Ferret-UI 2 averages 2.948, beating GUI-Vid (2.767, the benchmark's own model) and Ferret-UI (2.638) on iOS/Android/Web without overfitting (still below Gemini-1.5-Pro 3.295 and GPT-4o 3.619).
- <Finding src="Tab.4">Cross-platform transfer follows resolution/aspect-ratio similarity</Finding>: iPhone↔iPad↔Android transfer well (≈65–72), but AppleTV and Web (landscape) transfer poorly to mobile, and every other domain scores ≤~43 when tested on AppleTV — a large content/orientation domain gap.
- <Finding src="Tab.4">Data diversity aids generalization</Finding>: iPhone, trained on the largest set (~112k images), is the best donor platform, transferring at ≥68.1 (refer) / ≥63.1 (ground) to iPad and Android.
- <Finding src="Tab.7">Grounding transfers to unseen data within supported platform families</Finding>: on ScreenSpot (unseen), Ferret-UI 2 (8B) hits 54.0% avg, topping CogAgent (47.4%) and SeeClick (53.4%), strong on Mobile/Web but weak on the unseen Desktop split (e.g. 21.7% icon/widget).
- <Finding src="Tab.6">OCR, widget listing, and comprehensive description are the hardest subtasks</Finding> within their task types (e.g. OCR 69.3–80.5 across platforms vs find-widget ≥86), and even Gemma-2B (6× smaller than Vicuna-13B) stays competitive across the board.

## Implications
Establishes a recipe for universal UI perception: cost-bounded native-resolution encoding plus visually-grounded synthetic supervision. Extends the <Related>Ferret / Ferret-UI / Ferret-v2 referring-and-grounding lineage</Related> and connects to the broader computer-use-agent and multi-platform-benchmark line (<Related>CogAgent, SeeClick, GUI-World, GUIDE</Related>). <FollowUp>Single-step interaction only — chaining into a full multi-step planning agent is the obvious next step</FollowUp>; <FollowUp>the AppleTV/desktop domain gap suggests landscape-heavy and OS-desktop data are the next frontier</FollowUp>; <FollowUp>SoM-prompted GPT-4o as a data engine could generalize beyond UIs</FollowUp>.

## Limitations
- <Caveat>Single-step interaction only</Caveat>: the model responds to one screen at a time; multi-step navigation is shown only qualitatively (Appendix H/Fig.15), not benchmarked.
- <Caveat>Severe platform imbalance</Caveat> (Web 321k vs AppleTV 16k, iPad 19k) patched with loss weights/task-count heuristics rather than balanced collection.
- <Caveat>Advanced-task evaluation is GPT-4o-as-judge</Caveat>, and the same family (GPT-4o+SoM) generates the training data — a potential circularity and judge-bias risk.
- <Caveat>English-only</Caveat> by construction (>5% non-ASCII screenshots discarded); no multilingual UI support.
- <Caveat>Text labels come from OCR (conf ≥ 0.5)</Caveat> rather than ground truth on Apple platforms, so OCR errors propagate into supervision; OCR is itself the weakest subtask.
- <Caveat>Weak transfer to unseen Desktop/landscape</Caveat> (ScreenSpot desktop, AppleTV) — "universal" holds within trained platform families, less so outside them.

## Critiques and Questions
1. The headline 89.73 is a GPT-4o-judged score on the authors' own benchmark with GPT-4o-generated data; how much of the +12 over GPT-4o reflects genuine capability vs. distribution match to the judge?
2. Adaptive N-gridding's gain is small in isolation (Tab.5) — is the costlier search worth it over plain AnyRes given that data is the real driver?
3. Numbers appear to be single runs with no variance/error bars reported; the cross-platform transfer table especially would benefit from repeats.
4. Tappability is "-" for AppleTV (Tab.6) and SoM/GPT-4o rows have gaps (Tab.2) — coverage is uneven across the claimed 5 platforms.

## Glossary
- <Term def="Referring = answering questions about a widget given its location (OCR, classify, tappability); grounding = locating a widget given a description (listing, find-text, find-widget).">Referring vs grounding</Term>
- <Term def="Drawing numbered, color-coded marks/boxes directly on an image so a vision-LLM can reference specific regions by tag (Yang et al. 2023).">Set-of-Mark (SoM)</Term>
- <Term def="Any-Resolution: tile a high-res image into sub-images plus a low-res global view so a fixed CLIP encoder can perceive detail (LLaVA-NeXT).">AnyRes</Term>
- <Term def="Match predicted to ground-truth boxes, then average IoU per pair (IoU=0 if unmatched) — measures grounding in multi-widget answers.">Multi-IoU</Term>
- <Term def="The deduped, label-unified multi-platform screenshot dataset (529k samples) the authors build elementary and advanced tasks on.">Core-set</Term>
- <Term def="Web-based next-action-prediction benchmark (Chawla et al. 2024) used to test interaction grounding via semantic similarity + IoU.">GUIDE</Term>
