AGUVIS is the first fully autonomous, pure-vision GUI agent: a single Qwen2-VL-based model that operates directly on screenshots with a unified pyautogui action space and explicit inner-monologue reasoning, reaching state-of-the-art on offline and online benchmarks without any closed-source model in the loop.

A foundational reference for the computer-use-agent track: it shows an open-weights model can do both grounding and planning end-to-end on vision alone, decoupling the field from GPT-4o-as-planner and from platform-specific HTML/accessibility-tree inputs.

Motivation

Autonomous GUI automation has been stuck against four coupled limitations. Most agents consume textual interface representations (HTML or accessibility trees), which run past 4K tokens and grow with interface complexity, raising cost and hurting generalization. Heterogeneous, platform-specific action spaces fragment training data across web/mobile/desktop and block cross-environment learning. Methods either lack reliable visual grounding or outsource reasoning to closed-source LLMs, creating a bottleneck that cannot be improved by training. And agents are typically trained to emit reactive low-level actions directly, failing to exploit the planning capacity of VLMs.

Contribution (what’s new)

The genuine delta is a single open model that is both grounder and planner on vision alone. Three design choices make it work: a unified pyautogui-based action space with a plugin layer for platform-specific gestures, so one model’s “thought-to-action” mapping transfers across web, mobile, and desktop; explicit two-component inner monologue (a free-form thought plus a low-level natural-language instruction) baked into training data, turning reactive action prediction into deliberate planning; and a two-stage pipeline that first trains grounding on single screenshots, then trains planning/reasoning on inner-monologue trajectories. The vision-only observation is what permits the unified action space: pixel coordinates are a common substrate across platforms in a way HTML/accessibility trees are not.

Research questions

Can an open-weights, pure-vision model perform both grounding and high-level planning end-to-end across platforms, matching or beating pipelines that depend on closed-source planners and textual inputs — and does explicit inner-monologue reasoning, plus cross-platform unification, measurably help?

Methodology

AGUVIS finetunes Qwen2-VL (a NaViT-style dynamic-resolution encoder with 2D-RoPE, chosen for native variable-resolution screenshot handling) on a purpose-built dataset, then evaluates as either a standalone agent or a grounder paired with a GPT-4o planner.

Method details
  1. Unify observation + action — Drop HTML/accessibility-tree inputs; feed raw screenshots. All actions are expressed as pyautogui calls (e.g. pyautogui.click(x,y), pyautogui.write(...)). A plugin system adds platform-specific actions (mobile gestures, browser.select_option, terminate(status)) while keeping the base command grammar consistent (Tab.9).
  2. Build AGUVIS Data Collection — Grounding split: unify existing GUI datasets into pyautogui format and template-augment UI-element metadata into synthetic instruction-action pairs — 1.036M single-step trajectories across web/mobile/desktop (Tab.10). Planning split: prompt GPT-4o to add inner-monologue (thought + low-level instruction) onto existing trajectories, keeping prior instructions in context so reasoning is predictive, not post-hoc — ~35K multi-step trajectories (Tab.11).
  3. Stage 1 — Grounding training — Train on single screenshots with a 'grounding packing' strategy that bundles multiple instruction-action pairs per image (single-image-multiple-turn) to cut redundant training. Result: AGUVIS-G.
  4. Stage 2 — Planning & reasoning training — Continue from AGUVIS-G on inner-monologue trajectories with a reasoning mixture spanning bare action instructions up to full thought+plan monologues. Result: AGUVIS (7B and 72B).

The inner monologue at inference has two parts per step: an explicit thought h_t conditioned on the goal G and previous thought h_{t-1}, then a low-level natural-language action instruction that is translated into a concrete pyautogui command. The framework’s model-agnostic claim is checked by re-implementing on LLaVA-OneVision (§4.1).

Main result

On ScreenSpot grounding, AGUVIS-72B reaches 89.2% average, beating the strongest baseline (GPT-4o + UGround, 81.4%) and surpassing closed-source-planner pipelines (Tab.1); even the 7B model scores 84.4%. Across benchmarks AGUVIS leads on both grounding and end-to-end planning:

Key findings

  • On Multimodal-Mind2Web, AGUVIS lifts Step Success Rate by +51.9% on average over prior methods (Tab.2): the largest relative gains are in planning-heavy step success, not just element accuracy, indicating the reasoning stage, not only grounding, drives the win.
  • Despite being trained only on web and mobile data, AGUVIS generalizes to desktop OSWorld — 17.04% (GPT-4o planner + AGUVIS-7B) vs 4.59% for SoM and 14.9% for Claude Computer-Use; standalone AGUVIS-72B reaches 10.26% (Tab.8): evidence it captures transferable GUI principles rather than overfitting environments.
  • Both stages matter: removing Stage 2 (planning) drops ScreenSpot 84.4→81.8 and MM-Mind2Web Cross-Task 60.4→50.9; removing Stage 1 (grounding) drops ScreenSpot 84.4→77.4; removing both collapses ScreenSpot to 55.3 (Tab.6).
  • Inner monologue helps low-level execution, not just planning: removing it drops ScreenSpot 84.4→79.3 and AndroidControl low-level 80.5→69.1 (Tab.6) — a counterintuitive result, since one might expect reasoning to aid only high-level steps.
  • Unified cross-platform training beats platform-specific training: web+mobile data (35K traj.) scores 58.5 / 55.4 / 54.8 on MM-Mind2Web Task/Website/Domain vs 53.1 / 50.3 / 52.2 for web-only (6K) (Tab.7), supporting the shared-representation hypothesis behind the unified action space.
  • Pure vision cuts cost sharply: a constant 1,196 input tokens/step for 720p images vs ~4K for HTML, giving 93% cost reduction and 70% fewer input tokens/step vs GPT-4o on Mind2Web-Live (§4.4); cost-per-successful-step falls to $0.012 for AGUVIS-72B vs $0.142 for GPT-4o (Tab.4).
  • The pipeline is largely model-agnostic: on a weaker LLaVA backbone, ScreenSpot rises from 3.8% to 81.2% after full AGUVIS training (Tab.15), attributing gains to the method rather than to Qwen2-VL alone.

Implications

This reframes the computer-use-agent stack around an open, vision-only model that is simultaneously grounder and planner, undercutting the dominant GPT-4o-planner + separate-grounder pipelines such as UGround, SeeClick, and OmniParser on both accuracy and cost. It extends the lineage of vision-grounding work (SeeClick, UGround) and reasoning-augmented agents (inner monologue, Huang et al. 2022) into a single trainable artifact, and the open-sourced datasets/recipes lower the barrier for the field. Threads worth chasing: whether the inner-monologue augmentation can be regenerated with open VLMs instead of GPT-4o, removing the last closed-source dependency in the data pipeline (authors flag this as future work); adding refusal/uncertainty behavior so agents can decline ambiguous or unsafe actions; and a dynamic “when to plan” threshold to balance reasoning overhead against accuracy.

Limitations

  • The planning/reasoning training data depends on GPT-4o to synthesize inner monologues, so the “no closed-source model” claim holds at inference but not for data construction.
  • The agent cannot express uncertainty or refuse ambiguous instructions; in a 50-sample ScreenSpot error analysis, 40% of failures stem from ambiguous instructions and 60% from grounding errors (§4.5, Fig.4).
  • Standalone AGUVIS-72B underperforms its own GPT-4o-paired 7B configuration on some online tasks (e.g. AndroidWorld 26.1 vs 37.1, Tab.5), so the unified model is not yet uniformly the best deployment.
  • Online success rates are low in absolute terms (Mind2Web-Live 27.1%, OSWorld 17.04%), so real-world reliability remains far from usable.
  • Desktop generalization is shown only via OSWorld with no desktop training data; broad desktop competence is inferred, not directly trained or measured at scale.

Critiques and Questions

  1. The +51.9% Step-SR headline (Tab.2) is a relative average over heterogeneous baselines; the absolute Cross-Task gap is 64.0 vs 40.2, which is strong but less dramatic than the framing suggests — worth reading per-cell rather than as one number.
  2. Numbers appear to be single runs with no variance/error bars reported; given small online benchmarks (e.g. Mind2Web-Live, OSWorld’s 369 tasks), the ~3-point online margins over baselines may be within noise.
  3. Self-plan vs original-instruction ScreenSpot results are mixed in the table; it is worth confirming which setting each cited number comes from, since the unified action space’s advantage could partly reflect evaluation-protocol choices.
Why does the pure-vision design enable a unified cross-platform action space, where HTML/accessibility-tree inputs do not?
Pixel coordinates from a screenshot are a common substrate across web, mobile, and desktop, so a single pyautogui action grammar (e.g. click(x,y)) maps onto all of them; HTML and accessibility trees are platform-specific representations that fragment the action space and prevent cross-environment learning (§2.2, Tab.7).
